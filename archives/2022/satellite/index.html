The BirdSO Satellite Invitational for Divisions B and C ran from February 12–19, 2022. Featuring twenty-two official events, five trial events, and five events highlighted by our Computer Science Initiative, the tournament took place in a virtual format where students competed at their schools under the supervision of their coaches. The tournament kicked off with an opening ceremony and concluded with an awards ceremony where we awarded medals for the top fifteen places and trophies for the top fifteen teams.

Format

The Satellite Tournament was run in the Satellite SO format, described in the Rules Manual. Students competed at their schools under the supervision of their coaches. In addition, our tournament was asynchronous; teams could participate in any of the events at any given 50-minute interval during the duration of the competition (February 12–19, 2022). Teams from the same school were encouraged to take tests at the same time to ensure academic integrity.

Events

The event slate for the Satellite Tournament featured twenty-two official events, five trial events, and five Computer Science Initiative events. Events tried to follow the official Rules Manual; however, some modifications were necessary to adapt to our virtual format. Competitors were encouraged to review sections of the Event Modifications documents relevant to their events prior to the tournament.

Event Slate Event Modifications (Division B) Event Modifications (Division C) Trial + CS Event Rules

Computer Science (CS) Initiative: Considering the increasing prevalence of Computer Science both as a specialized skill and as a tool used across education and science careers, BirdSO supplemented the Science Olympiad event slate with five additional events that we hoped would help offer additional avenues to explore this burgeoning field. For Division B, we ran Code Analysis, Cybersecurity, Game On, Run It Code It, and Spreadsheet Scripting. For Division C, we ran Bioinformatics, Cybersecurity, Data Science, Machine Learning, and Run It Code It. Many of these events were developed with the help of our BirdSO team and were also run at the 2022 National Tournament. We were excited to help facillitate the debut of these events during the 2021–2022 season!

Policies

All competitors were expected to follow Science Olympiad rules (both event-specific and General Rules), our Academic Honesty Policy, and the additional general policies included below.

  • All notesheets, for events that allow 8.5 by 11 inch sheet(s) of paper, must be printed out. For these events as well as events that do not allow any external resources, event supervisors will keep track of "time out of browser."
  • Satellite Only: For events that allow a binder, binders must be printed out.
  • Satellite Only: Participants may use any method of communication such as video call, voice chat, the built-in Scilympiad chat. Partners are allowed to communicate in-person with their partner(s) while participating in events.
  • All test events are 50 minutes long.
  • No-shows will result in [N + 1] points. Disqualifications as a result of cheating will result in [N + 2] points.
  • Events will follow national-level rules if applicable.
  • All video submissions and practice logs will be properly disposed of two weeks (14 days) after competition. No build videos will be saved.

Ceremonies

The Satellite Tournament featured a virtual Opening Ceremony on February 11, 2022, at 5:00 PM Pacific Time to kickoff our exciting week of competition. After the competition concluded, we hosted a virtual Awards Ceremony on February 26, 2022, at 5:00 PM Pacific Time where we recognized the top finishers of both divisions in each event and overall. After the Awards Ceremony, medals and trophies were mailed to coaches, and graded tests were made available for study on Scilympiad.

Links to our ceremonies, which were broadcasted on our YouTube channel, were shared here ahead of each. We hope these archives will allow your teams to relive some of the excitement from these exciting events!
